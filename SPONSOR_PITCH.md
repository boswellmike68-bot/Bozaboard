# Bozaboard — Founding Sponsor Pitch

## The Problem

AI systems are making decisions that affect people's lives — but there's no transparency, no audit trail, and no way to challenge the output. Governance is an afterthought bolted on after harm is done.

## The Solution

**Bozaboard** is a zero-dependency, accessibility-first governance UI that puts transparent decision-making at the center. It's powered by the **BBnCC governance engine** — a deterministic advisory system with:

- **Intent classification** — every input is categorized, never guessed
- **7-rule weighted governance engine** — stability, escalation, contradiction, oscillation, repetition, reinforcement, and session depth rules fire by authority rank
- **4 Committee personas** — Stability Chair, Momentum Chair, Risk Chair, Alignment Chair respond based on real governance state
- **Momentum tracking** — session-level momentum model drives visual consensus ring
- **Full audit logging** — every AI action timestamped and inspectable
- **Zero external calls** — nothing leaves the browser, ever

## What Makes This Different

| Traditional AI | Bozaboard |
|---------------|-----------|
| Black box decisions | Every rule is visible and documented |
| No audit trail | Timestamped log of every action |
| Requires internet/API keys | Runs entirely offline |
| Framework-heavy, complex builds | Zero dependencies, single HTML file |
| Accessibility bolted on | Accessibility is the foundation |
| Emotional manipulation tolerated | Neutrality protocol enforced |

## Who This Helps

- **Families rebuilding after crisis** — governed systems that don't exploit vulnerability
- **Organizations needing transparent AI** — auditable, deterministic advisory
- **Developers seeking governance templates** — modular, open architecture
- **Communities demanding accountability** — no hidden processes, no black boxes

## The Story

Bozaboard was built by a post-fire family rebuilding through governed technology. Every design decision — high contrast, zero telemetry, deterministic behavior, offline-first — comes from lived experience with systems that failed when people needed them most.

This isn't theoretical governance. It's governance born from necessity.
